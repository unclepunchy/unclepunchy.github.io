Learning how to code

My first kid was born a week into the COVID-19 shutdown. It was an exciting and scary time. I had a lot to learn about taking care of a baby, and the added stress of living through a global pandemic was not helpful. One nice thing was that the baby preferred to sleep laying on my chest for the first few months of her life. I think it was comforting for both of us, and I feel fortunate to have built a strong bond with my daughter. At the same time, it was a bit of an endurance game. I spent hours sitting as still and quietly as I could while she snoozed on my chest. I've never been great at being idle, so enevitably my mind would wander.

TV, podcasts, and audiobooks are all awesome, but eventually I felt like I needed to do something more productive. Making things has always been a huge part of my life, and being away from work for so long was really hard. I needed something I could do with a baby sleeping on my chest. That's when I started looking for apps that I could use to teach myself how to code. I came across Mimo and I got hoooked. I've also been learning on Free Code Camp and playing around with Atom. I think these resources have been pretty helpful. I can learn and practice on my phone with Mimo, and get a little more in depth on my computer with Free Code Camp and Atom. I'm starting out with HTML and CSS. I'll move on to JavaScript next. I'm really enjoying it. I think there's a lot of connection for me between coding and being a craftperson who works with his hands. I love making things just right and the process it takes to get there. Now, I have ambitions of eventually playing around with Linux and Python using a Raspberry Pi. I'm also really excited about being able to get my daughter involved with technology when she's older. Who knows? Maybe I'll even be able to make some money at this eventually.
